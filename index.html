Asiye Kaymaz Özcanlı

About Me | Research | Teaching | Publications | People

I am Asiye Kaymaz Özcanlı, an Assistant Professor in the Department of Electrical and Electronics Engineering at Fatih Sultan Mehmet Vakıf University. I recently served as a visiting post-doc researcher (2024–2025) in the Department of Engineering and Public Policy at Carnegie Mellon University through the TÜBİTAK 2219 postdoctoral fellowship program, where I worked with Prof. Ramteen Sioshansi on projects examining how electric-vehicle batteries can enhance electricity-supply resilience for residential customers.

I received my B.S. and double-major degree in Electrical Engineering and Mechanical Engineering from Yıldız Technical University in 2010, followed by my M.S. and Ph.D. degrees in Electrical Engineering in 2016 and 2022. My broader research interests include renewable-energy integration, microgrids, outage prediction, machine-learning-based energy forecasting, battery-storage modeling, and developing data-driven tools for modern power-system applications.
